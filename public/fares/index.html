Fare Structure

Metrorail uses a distance-based fare system. The further you travel, the more you pay. Fares also vary by time of day — peak fares apply during rush hours on weekdays, while off-peak fares apply at all other times.

Fare Category Peak Off-Peak Senior/Disabled
Boarding (minimum fare) $2.25 $2.00 $1.10
Short trip (1-3 miles) $2.25 - $2.75 $2.00 $1.10
Medium trip (3-6 miles) $2.75 - $3.75 $2.00 - $2.50 $1.35 - $1.85
Long trip (6+ miles) $3.75 - $6.00 $2.50 - $3.85 $1.85 - $2.95
Maximum fare $6.00 $3.85 $2.95

Peak Hours

Peak fares are charged during weekday rush hours. Off-peak fares apply at all other times, including federal holidays regardless of the day of the week.

When Peak Fares Apply
Weekdays Opening - 9:30 AM & 3:00 PM - 7:00 PM
Weekends Off-Peak All Day
Federal Holidays Off-Peak All Day

Transfers & SmarTrip

Transfer Rules

  • Rail-to-rail transfers are included in your fare during a continuous trip
  • Rail-to-bus transfers give a $0.50 discount on the connecting Metrobus fare with SmarTrip
  • Bus-to-rail transfers do not receive a discount
  • Transfers are valid for up to 2 hours from the start of your trip

SmarTrip Card

A SmarTrip card or contactless payment is required to ride Metrorail. Cards cost $2.00 and can be purchased at:

  • Fare machines in every Metro station
  • Select retail locations
  • Online at wmata.com

You can also tap a contactless credit/debit card or mobile wallet (Apple Pay, Google Pay) at fare gates.

Fares shown are sourced from the WMATA API and may not reflect temporary promotions or fare changes. For the most current fare information, visit wmata.com/fares. NextMetro is not affiliated with WMATA.
