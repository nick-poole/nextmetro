    Fare Structure

    Metrorail uses a distance-based fare system. The further you travel, the more you pay. Fares also vary by time of day — peak fares apply during rush hours on weekdays, while off-peak fares apply at all other times.

    Fare Category Weekday Late Night / Weekend Senior/Disabled
    Boarding (minimum fare) $2.25 $2.25 $1.10
    Short trip (1-3 miles) $2.25 - $3.00 $2.25 $1.10
    Medium trip (3-6 miles) $3.00 - $4.50 $2.25 - $2.50 $1.10 - $2.25
    Long trip (6+ miles) $4.50 - $6.75 $2.50 $2.25 - $3.35
    Maximum fare $6.75 $2.50 $3.35

    Fare Hours

    Weekday fares are distance-based. Late night (after 9:30 PM) and weekend fares use a reduced flat rate. Federal holidays use weekend fare rates.

    When Fare Rates Apply
    Weekdays (opening–9:30 PM) $2.25 – $6.75 (distance-based)
    Late Night (after 9:30 PM) $2.25 – $2.50
    Weekends & Holidays $2.25 – $2.50

    For full operating hours, train frequency, and holiday schedules, see Metro hours & schedule.

    Transfers & SmarTrip

    Transfer Rules

    • Rail-to-rail transfers are included in your fare during a continuous trip
    • Rail-to-bus and bus-to-rail transfers are free ($2.25 discount) within 2 hours with SmarTrip
    • Bus-to-bus transfers are free with unlimited transfers within 2 hours
    • Transfers are valid for up to 2 hours from the start of your trip

    SmarTrip Card

    A SmarTrip card or contactless payment is required to ride Metrorail. Cards cost $2.00 and can be purchased at:

    • Fare machines in every Metro station
    • Select retail locations
    • Online at wmata.com

    You can also tap a contactless credit/debit card or mobile wallet (Apple Pay, Google Pay) at fare gates.
